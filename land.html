Geriatric Cancer Patient Assessment

Welcome to the Geriatric Cancer Patient Assessment... You are one step away form optimizing your health and well-being.

Geriatric assessment is a comprehensive evaluation of an older adult's overall health status, functional abilities, social support, and other relevant factors. It involves a multidimensional approach to assess various aspects of an older individual's physical, mental, and social well-being. The primary goal of a geriatric assessment is to develop a tailored care plan that addresses the specific needs and challenges of the older adult.

This assessment is designed to evaluate the needs and conditions of geriatric cancer patients. It provides a comprehensive analysis of various aspects related to the patient's health and well-being.

Please proceed to the "Patient Information" section to enter the necessary details for assessment.

Geriatric cancer refers to cancer that occurs in elderly individuals, typically those aged 65 years and older. As people age, the risk of developing cancer increases, and geriatric cancer becomes more prevalent due to various factors, including cumulative exposure to environmental factors, genetic predisposition, and age-related changes in the body's cells.

While lung cancer can affect people of all ages, it is more frequently diagnosed in older individuals due to the cumulative effects of tobacco smoke exposure.

Colorectal cancer affects the colon or rectum and is more common in older adults. Regular screening is important to detect and prevent this type of cancer.

Pancreatic cancer is more common in older adults, with the average age of diagnosis being 70 years. It tends to be aggressive and is often diagnosed at advanced stages.

Kidney cancer, specifically renal cell carcinoma, is more prevalent in older individuals. It typically affects people between the ages of 50 and 70, with the risk increasing with age.

Bladder cancer incidence increases with age, and it is more commonly diagnosed in older adults, especially in those with a history of smoking or occupational exposure to certain chemicals.

Why Geriatric assessment?

Comprehensive Evaluation: It provides a holistic view of an older adult's health, taking into account various physical, cognitive, functional, and psychosocial factors that can impact their well-being.

Individualized Care Planning: The assessment helps identify specific areas of concern and develops tailored care plans that address the unique needs and preferences of the older adult. It can guide appropriate interventions and support services.

Early Detection of Issues: Geriatric assessment can help detect health problems, functional impairments, or cognitive decline at early stages when interventions may be more effective in preventing or managing these conditions.

Care Coordination: It facilitates coordination among healthcare professionals, caregivers, and support services to ensure a collaborative approach to the care of older adults.

Prognostic Information: Geriatric assessment can provide prognostic information about an individual's overall health status, functional abilities, and potential risks, aiding in shared decision-making and planning for future care.
